The collection of scientific articles highlights the transformative role of CRISPR technology in genomics and biotechnology, showcasing its diverse applications and potential for innovation. In the realm of gene editing, the article on porcine embryos discusses various methodologies like somatic cell nuclear transfer and microinjection, emphasizing the need to refine these techniques to reduce off-target effects and mosaicism, which are crucial for advancing agricultural and biomedical applications.

In diagnostics, the integration of CRISPR-Cas12a with DNAzyme-triggered biosensors offers a simplified, one-pot approach for nucleic acid detection, addressing current limitations in efficiency and complexity. This advancement could revolutionize diagnostic procedures by enhancing the accuracy and ease of nucleic acid testing.

The exploration of bacteriophages engineered with CRISPR-Cas systems presents a novel strategy to combat antibiotic-resistant superbugs. By precisely manipulating phage genomes, researchers aim to develop phage therapies with enhanced specificity and efficacy, offering a promising alternative to traditional antibiotics.

In plant genomics, CRISPR-mediated prime editing in rice demonstrates significant potential for gene tagging and functional studies. The development of the NM-PE toolkit and the EXPERT system expands the editing capabilities, allowing for more efficient and precise genetic modifications, which are vital for crop improvement and functional genomics.

The use of CRISPR in base editing is further advanced by harnessing novel cytidine deaminases, broadening the scope of C-to-T conversions in rice with minimal off-target effects. This innovation enhances the precision and applicability of base editing in plant biotechnology.

In medical biotechnology, CRISPR-Cas9 editing of the BCL11B gene in CAR-engineered lymphoid progenitors shows promise in developing NK-like cells with prolonged anti-leukemic activity, offering insights into tailoring immune cell therapies for cancer treatment.

Lastly, CRISPR-Cas9's application in fungal genomics, specifically in Fusarium circinatum, reveals the role of the Pgs gene in pathogenicity, growth, and sporulation, paving the way for functional gene characterization and potential disease control strategies. These studies collectively underscore CRISPR's pivotal role in advancing genomics and biotechnology across various fields.

The collection of scientific articles highlights the transformative role of CRISPR technology in genomics and biotechnology, showcasing its diverse applications and potential advancements. In the realm of gene editing in porcine embryos, CRISPR/Cas9 is pivotal for biomedical and agricultural research, with methodologies like SCNT, microinjection, and electroporation being explored. Despite challenges such as off-target effects and mosaicism, refining these techniques could enhance agricultural and biomedical applications.

In diagnostics, a novel CRISPR-Cas12a biosensor integrated with DNAzyme-triggered equilibrium transfer simplifies nucleic acid detection, promising more efficient diagnostic procedures. Meanwhile, CRISPR's role in combating antibiotic-resistant superbugs is underscored by engineering bacteriophages for targeted bacterial eradication, with CRISPR-Cas systems enhancing phage genome manipulation for improved specificity and efficacy.

In plant genomics, CRISPR/Cas-mediated prime editing in rice facilitates efficient in situ epitope tagging, broadening the scope for gene function studies and genetic improvements. Similarly, novel cytidine deaminases from the animal kingdom have been harnessed for robust multiplexed base editing in rice, expanding the targeting scope and minimizing off-target effects.

The EXPERT system extends prime editing capabilities, allowing for large fragment edits with enhanced efficiency and minimal off-target effects, showcasing significant potential in life sciences. In immunotherapy, CRISPR/Cas9-mediated Bcl11b knockout in CAR-engineered lymphoid progenitors enhances anti-leukemic activity, offering insights into tailoring CAR cell functionality.

Finally, CRISPR-Cas9 genome editing in Fusarium circinatum reveals the Pgs gene's role in pathogenicity, growth, and sporulation, advancing our understanding of this destructive pathogen. Collectively, these studies underscore CRISPR's versatility and potential to revolutionize genomics and biotechnology, driving innovations across various fields.

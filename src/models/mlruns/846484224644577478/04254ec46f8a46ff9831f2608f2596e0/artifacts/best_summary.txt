The scientific articles reviewed focus on the integration of agent-based frameworks and artificial intelligence (AI) models in various domains, highlighting their methodologies, key findings, and implications.

The article "Leveraging Large Language Models and Agent-Based Systems for Scientific Data Analysis: Validation Study" explores the use of large language models (LLMs) in scientific data analysis. The study introduces the LITURAt system, which employs an agent-based architecture to enhance the factual accuracy and domain-specific precision of LLMs. By integrating external data retrieval and analysis tools, LITURAt ensures context-aware outputs, making scientific information more accessible to both experts and nonexperts. This approach addresses the limitations of LLMs in scientific domains, emphasizing the potential of agent-based systems to improve AI model reliability and applicability.

In "Measuring water pollution effects on antimicrobial resistance through explainable artificial intelligence," the authors utilize explainable AI to assess the impact of water pollution on antimicrobial resistance. The study employs the One Health framework to analyze various indicators affecting antimicrobial resistance across different countries. The findings underscore the critical role of water quality and accessibility in determining mortality rates due to resistant pathogens. This research highlights the importance of AI frameworks in identifying key environmental factors and supporting decision-making processes in public health.

The "Smart Pharmaceutical Monitoring System With Personalized Medication Schedules and Self-Management Programs for Patients With Diabetes" article discusses the development of a smart monitoring system for diabetes management. The system leverages AI and digital technologies to create personalized medication schedules and self-management programs, addressing challenges such as treatment complexity and patient adherence. This study demonstrates the potential of AI frameworks to enhance healthcare delivery and patient outcomes in chronic disease management.

Overall, these articles illustrate the diverse applications of agent-based frameworks and AI models in scientific research, healthcare, and environmental studies, showcasing their ability to improve data analysis, decision-making, and personalized interventions.

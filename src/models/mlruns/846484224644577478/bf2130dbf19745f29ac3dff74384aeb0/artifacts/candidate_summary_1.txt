The compilation of scientific articles highlights the transformative impact of CRISPR technology across various domains of genomics and biotechnology. In the realm of genetic modification, the use of CRISPR/Cas9 in porcine embryos is explored, revealing the potential of different delivery methods like SCNT and microinjection, alongside innovative approaches like SMGT and TMGT. Despite challenges such as off-target effects and mosaicism, advancements in these methods could significantly benefit agricultural and biomedical applications.

In diagnostics, a novel DNAzyme-triggered CRISPR-Cas12a biosensor is developed, integrating recombinase-polymerase amplification (RPA) to enhance nucleic acid detection, simplifying diagnostic processes significantly. Meanwhile, CRISPR-Cas systems are also leveraged in bacteriophage engineering, targeting antibiotic-resistant superbugs. This approach underscores the potential of CRISPR in creating phage therapies with increased specificity and efficacy, although ethical considerations remain.

In agriculture, CRISPR-mediated prime editing in rice demonstrates significant advancements. The NM-PE strategy facilitates efficient epitope tagging in rice, broadening the scope for genetic studies and improvements. Similarly, the EXPERT system extends prime editing capabilities, allowing for large fragment edits with enhanced efficiency and safety.

Further, the exploration of novel cytidine deaminases expands the CRISPR-Cas base editing toolkit, improving precision and reducing off-target effects in rice, thus enhancing the potential for genetic improvements. In another study, CRISPR-Cas9 editing elucidates the role of the Pgs gene in the pathogenicity of Fusarium circinatum, highlighting the utility of CRISPR in functional genomic studies.

Lastly, the targeted editing of BCL11B in CAR-engineered lymphoid progenitors showcases CRISPR's role in immunotherapy, enhancing anti-leukemic responses. Collectively, these studies underscore CRISPR's vast potential in revolutionizing biotechnology and genomics, paving the way for innovative applications across diverse fields.

The collection of scientific articles highlights the transformative role of CRISPR technology in genomics and biotechnology, showcasing its diverse applications and potential for innovation. In the realm of gene editing in porcine embryos, CRISPR/Cas9 is pivotal for advancing biomedical and agricultural research. The review emphasizes methodologies like somatic cell nuclear transfer and microinjection, while addressing challenges such as off-target effects and mosaicism, underscoring the need for refined techniques to enhance accuracy and reduce mosaicism.

In diagnostics, a novel DNAzyme-triggered CRISPR-Cas12a biosensor integrates recombinase-polymerase amplification to streamline nucleic acid detection, promising simplified and efficient diagnostic procedures. Meanwhile, the engineering of bacteriophages using CRISPR-Cas systems offers a promising strategy against antibiotic-resistant superbugs, enhancing phage specificity and efficacy.

In plant genomics, CRISPR/Cas-mediated prime editing facilitates efficient in situ epitope tagging in rice, broadening the scope of gene function studies and genetic improvement. The development of the EXPERT system extends prime editing capabilities, enabling large fragment edits with enhanced efficiency and minimal off-target effects, showcasing significant potential in life sciences.

CRISPR technology also advances cancer immunotherapy, as demonstrated by targeting BCL11B in CAR-engineered lymphoid progenitors, which drives NK-like cell development with prolonged anti-leukemic activity. In rice, prime editing restores the function of the rc allele, highlighting its precision in correcting genetic mutations.

Lastly, CRISPR-Cas9-mediated genome editing in Fusarium circinatum reveals the Pgs gene's role in pathogenicity, growth, and sporulation, offering insights into the molecular basis of fungal pathogenicity and opening new avenues for functional gene characterization.

Collectively, these studies underscore CRISPR's versatility and potential to revolutionize genomics and biotechnology, driving advancements in agriculture, medicine, and pathogen research.

The reviewed scientific articles collectively explore the transformative potential of CRISPR technology across various domains, emphasizing its applications in genomics and biotechnology. The article on gene editing in porcine embryos highlights CRISPR/Cas9 as a pivotal tool in genetic modification, addressing methodologies like SCNT, microinjection, and electroporation, while acknowledging challenges such as off-target effects and mosaicism. Refinement of these techniques could enhance agricultural and biomedical applications.

In diagnostics, a DNAzyme-triggered CRISPR-Cas12a biosensor simplifies nucleic acid detection, integrating recombinase-polymerase amplification to potentially streamline diagnostic procedures. Similarly, the engineering of bacteriophages using CRISPR for targeting antibiotic-resistant bacteria presents a novel approach to combat superbugs, emphasizing the precision of CRISPR in phage genome manipulation.

CRISPR's role in plant genomics is exemplified by a study on rice, where nuclease-mediated prime editing advances in situ epitope tagging, enhancing gene function studies and genetic improvements. Another rice-focused study introduces novel cytidine deaminases for robust base editing, expanding the genomic context and reducing off-target effects, thus broadening CRISPR's applicability in plant biotechnology.

The EXPERT system extends prime editing capabilities by allowing modifications beyond conventional pegRNA limits, demonstrating significant improvements in editing efficiency for large genomic fragments without increasing off-target effects. This advancement holds substantial promise for precise genomic modifications in biotechnology.

In medical biotechnology, CRISPR/Cas9-mediated Bcl11b knockout in CAR-engineered lymphoid progenitors fosters NK-like cell development, enhancing anti-leukemic activities, while another study uses CRISPR to elucidate the role of the Pgs gene in the pathogenicity of Fusarium circinatum, showcasing CRISPR's utility in functional genomics. Collectively, these studies underscore CRISPR's versatility and its profound implications for advancing genomics and biotechnological innovations.

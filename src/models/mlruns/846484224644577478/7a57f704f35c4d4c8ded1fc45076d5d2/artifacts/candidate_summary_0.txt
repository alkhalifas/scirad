The exploration of CRISPR technology in genomics and biotechnology has led to significant advancements across various fields, as demonstrated by recent studies. In porcine embryos, CRISPR/Cas9 has been pivotal for genetic modifications, with methodologies like SCNT, microinjection, and electroporation being assessed for efficiency and limitations. Challenges such as high off-target effects and mosaicism persist, necessitating improved precision for agricultural and biomedical applications.

In diagnostics, a novel CRISPR-Cas12a biosensor integrates with DNAzyme-triggered equilibrium transfer, simplifying nucleic acid detection. This advancement highlights CRISPR's potential in enhancing diagnostic accuracy and efficiency. Similarly, CRISPR's role in combating antibiotic-resistant superbugs is underscored by engineered bacteriophages. By manipulating phage genomes through CRISPR-Cas systems, these phages exhibit enhanced efficacy against resistant bacteria, indicating a promising avenue for addressing global health challenges.

In plant genomics, CRISPR-mediated prime editing has facilitated efficient epitope tagging in rice, broadening the scope for gene function studies and genetic improvements. The NM-PE strategy, utilizing SpCas9 and MMEJ, demonstrates enhanced tagging capabilities, illustrating CRISPR's transformative impact on plant biotechnology.

The development of EXPERT, an extended prime editor system, expands the editing range for large genomic fragments, significantly improving editing efficiency without increasing off-target effects. This advancement enhances CRISPR's applicability in complex genomic edits.

In medical biotechnology, targeting BCL11B in CAR-engineered lymphoid progenitors using CRISPR/Cas9 fosters NK-like cell development, enhancing anti-leukemic activity. This approach illustrates CRISPR's potential in developing targeted immunotherapies.

Additionally, CRISPR-Cas9 has been used to elucidate the role of the Pgs gene in Fusarium circinatum, a pathogen affecting pine species. The study highlights CRISPR's utility in functional genomics, aiding in understanding pathogen biology and developing disease management strategies.

Overall, these studies underscore CRISPR's versatility and transformative potential across genomics and biotechnology, paving the way for innovative solutions to complex biological challenges.

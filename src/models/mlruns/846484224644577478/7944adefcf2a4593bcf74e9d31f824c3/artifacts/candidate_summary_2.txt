The selected scientific articles explore various applications of agent-based frameworks and artificial intelligence in diverse fields, highlighting significant advancements and implications.

The study "Leveraging Large Language Models and Agent-Based Systems for Scientific Data Analysis" presents an innovative approach using the LITURAt system, which integrates agent-based architecture to enhance the precision and reliability of scientific data analysis. By employing external data retrieval and analysis tools, this system addresses the challenges of factual accuracy and domain-specific precision in large language models. This approach makes scientific information more accessible to both experts and nonexperts, suggesting a promising direction for AI applications in scientific research.

In the article "Measuring water pollution effects on antimicrobial resistance through explainable artificial intelligence," explainable AI is utilized within the One Health framework to analyze the impact of water pollution on antimicrobial resistance. The study identifies critical indicators, such as water accessibility and quality, that influence mortality due to antimicrobial resistance across different countries. This research underscores the potential of AI as a decision-support tool in public health and environmental monitoring.

The "Smart Pharmaceutical Monitoring System With Personalized Medication Schedules and Self-Management Programs for Patients With Diabetes" explores the development of an AI-driven system aimed at improving diabetes management. By addressing treatment complexity and patient adherence, this system offers personalized medication schedules and self-management programs, illustrating the transformative potential of AI in healthcare.

These studies collectively emphasize the role of agent-based frameworks and AI in enhancing the accuracy, accessibility, and applicability of data analysis across various domains. The methodologies employed demonstrate the integration of AI with domain-specific frameworks to address complex challenges, highlighting the implications for improving decision-making, healthcare outcomes, and scientific communication.

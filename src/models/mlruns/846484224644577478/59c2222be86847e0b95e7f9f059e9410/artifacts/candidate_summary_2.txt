The collection of scientific articles highlights the transformative role of CRISPR technology in genomics and biotechnology, showcasing its diverse applications and potential advancements. In the realm of gene editing in porcine embryos, CRISPR/Cas9 is pivotal for biomedical and agricultural research, with methodologies like SCNT, microinjection, and electroporation being explored. Despite challenges such as off-target effects and mosaicism, refining these techniques could enhance agricultural and biomedical applications.

In diagnostics, a novel CRISPR-Cas12a biosensor integrated with DNAzyme-triggered equilibrium transfer simplifies nucleic acid detection, promising more efficient diagnostic procedures. Meanwhile, CRISPR's role in combating antibiotic-resistant superbugs is underscored by engineering bacteriophages for targeted bacterial eradication, with CRISPR-Cas systems enhancing phage genome manipulation for improved specificity and efficacy.

In plant genomics, CRISPR-mediated prime editing in rice facilitates efficient in situ epitope tagging, broadening the scope for gene function studies and genetic improvements. Similarly, the development of novel cytidine deaminases for CRISPR-based base editing in rice expands editing capabilities, offering high efficiency and minimal off-target effects.

The EXPERT system advances prime editing by enabling large fragment edits, significantly enhancing editing efficiency without increasing off-target rates. In immunotherapy, CRISPR/Cas9-mediated Bcl11b knockout in CAR-engineered lymphoid progenitors enhances anti-leukemic activity, offering insights into tailoring immune responses.

Lastly, CRISPR-Cas9's application in fungal genomics reveals the Pgs gene's role in Fusarium circinatum's pathogenicity, growth, and sporulation, opening new avenues for functional gene characterization. Collectively, these studies underscore CRISPR's vast potential in genomics and biotechnology, driving innovations across various fields.

The collection of articles presents diverse advancements and applications of CRISPR technology across various fields. The first article discusses the establishment of a highly efficient multi-target CRISPR/Cas9 editing system for forestry trees, highlighting its potential for genetic improvement in magnoliid woody plants. The second article reviews the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) in optimizing CRISPR-Cas9 processes, addressing the gap between AI and CRISPR research to improve predictive performance and efficiency. The third article introduces CRISPuRe-seq, a novel screening strategy that links genotypes to RNA barcodes, revealing the integrated stress response's regulation of tRNA production via mTOR pathways. The fourth article explores non-viral delivery systems for CRISPR-Cas9 gene therapy, emphasizing stimuli-responsive nanoformulations to enhance targeting specificity and delivery efficiency. The fifth article demonstrates CRISPR-Cas13's potential in inhibiting RNA viruses, specifically targeting porcine epidemic diarrhoea virus replication. The sixth article reviews gene editing in porcine embryos, focusing on methodologies and challenges such as mosaicism and off-target effects. The seventh article examines CRISPR-Cas9's potential as a precise antimicrobial therapy against antimicrobial resistance by targeting resistant genes. The eighth article presents a CRISPRi approach for multi-locus screening of transcription factor binding sites using truncated guides. The ninth article discusses CRISPR-based epigenome editing to activate the imprinted Prader-Willi syndrome locus, offering potential therapeutic interventions. Finally, the tenth article details the construction of a TAT-Cas9-EGFP cell line using PEG10 modification to enhance site-specific integration efficiency, contributing to advancements in gene therapy and protein production.

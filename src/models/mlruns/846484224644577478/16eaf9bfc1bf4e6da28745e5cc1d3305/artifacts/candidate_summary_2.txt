The scientific articles reviewed offer insights into the application of agent-based frameworks and artificial intelligence (AI) methodologies across various domains. In "Leveraging Large Language Models and Agent-Based Systems for Scientific Data Analysis: Validation Study," the study highlights the development of LITURAt, an agent-based architecture designed to enhance the precision of large language models (LLMs) in scientific data analysis. By integrating external data retrieval and analysis tools, LITURAt addresses the challenges of factual accuracy and domain specificity in LLMs, making scientific information more accessible to diverse audiences. This approach underscores the potential of agent-based systems in refining AI outputs, enhancing their reliability and contextual awareness.

The article "Measuring water pollution effects on antimicrobial resistance through explainable artificial intelligence" employs an explainable AI framework to identify key factors influencing antimicrobial resistance (AMR). Utilizing the One Health framework, the study predicts mortality rates related to AMR pathogens, emphasizing the critical role of water quality indicators. This research demonstrates how AI can provide valuable decision-support tools for monitoring and addressing AMR, highlighting the importance of integrating environmental factors into health assessments.

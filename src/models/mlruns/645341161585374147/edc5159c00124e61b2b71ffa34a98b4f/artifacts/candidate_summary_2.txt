The collection of scientific articles highlights the transformative role of CRISPR technology in genomics and biotechnology, showcasing its diverse applications across various organisms and systems. In the realm of porcine embryos, CRISPR/Cas9 has been pivotal in genetic modifications, with methodologies like somatic cell nuclear transfer and microinjection being explored. Despite challenges such as off-target effects and mosaicism, advancements in these techniques are crucial for agricultural and biomedical applications.

In plant genomics, CRISPR-mediated prime editing has been effectively utilized for in situ epitope tagging in rice, enhancing gene function studies and genetic improvements. The development of the NM-PE strategy, which employs SpCas9 and microhomology-mediated end joining, has streamlined gene tagging processes, broadening the scope of genetic modifications in rice.

The exploration of novel cytidine deaminases for base editing in rice has expanded the capabilities of CRISPR-based cytosine base editors, allowing for precise C-to-T conversions with minimal off-target effects. This advancement holds promise for robust multiplexed base editing, crucial for genetic improvements in crops.

In synthetic biology, the development of a molecular proximity sensor using engineered guide RNA demonstrates the potential of CRISPR in designing molecular circuits for genome editing, paving the way for programmable biological systems.

The EXPERT system enhances prime editing by extending the editing range, allowing for large fragment edits with improved efficiency and safety. This innovation significantly advances the potential of prime editing in life sciences.

In medical biotechnology, CRISPR/Cas9-mediated editing of BCL11B in CAR-engineered lymphoid progenitors has shown promise in developing NK-like cells with prolonged anti-leukemic activity, offering insights into tailored cancer therapies.

CRISPR technology has also been applied to study pathogenicity in Fusarium circinatum, revealing the role of the Pgs gene in growth and sporulation, and in maize, where editing of the PMT1 gene has optimized lignin composition for biorefining applications.

Overall, these studies underscore the versatility and potential of CRISPR technology in advancing genomics and biotechnology, with significant implications for agriculture, medicine, and synthetic biology.

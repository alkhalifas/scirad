The selected scientific articles explore various applications of agent-based frameworks and artificial intelligence (AI) models, highlighting their methodologies, key findings, and implications.

1. **Leveraging Large Language Models and Agent-Based Systems for Scientific Data Analysis: Validation Study**: This study introduces the LITURAt system, which employs an agent-based architecture to enhance the application of large language models in scientific data analysis. By integrating external data retrieval and analysis tools, LITURAt addresses challenges related to factual accuracy and domain-specific precision, making scientific information more accessible. The key finding is that agent-based systems can significantly improve the reliability and context-awareness of AI outputs in scientific domains.

2. **Constraining an Unconstrained Multi-agent Policy with Offline Data**: The article presents CUTMAP, a novel method within the framework of Constrained Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (CMARL). CUTMAP addresses the limitations of online learning in multi-agent systems by using offline data to enforce constraints, ensuring safety and adaptability to new constraints. The methodology involves a scalable optimization objective and a decomposable Q-function, enhancing the practicality of multi-agent systems in real-world applications.

3. **Measuring Water Pollution Effects on Antimicrobial Resistance through Explainable Artificial Intelligence**: This research employs explainable AI to analyze the impact of water pollution on antimicrobial resistance. Using the One Health framework, the study identifies key indicators affecting resistance-related mortality across different countries. The findings underscore the critical role of water quality in antimicrobial resistance, offering insights for decision support and monitoring.

These articles collectively demonstrate the potential of agent-based frameworks and AI models in addressing complex challenges across various domains. The integration of agent-based systems enhances the precision and adaptability of AI applications, while explainable AI provides valuable insights for decision-making. These advancements have significant implications for improving scientific data analysis, multi-agent system safety, and public health strategies.

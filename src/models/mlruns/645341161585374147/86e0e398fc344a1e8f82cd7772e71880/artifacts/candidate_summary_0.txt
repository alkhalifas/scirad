The reviewed scientific articles highlight advancements in agent and multi-agent based frameworks for artificial intelligence (AI) models and systems, emphasizing key methodologies and implications.

1. **Leveraging Large Language Models and Agent-Based Systems for Scientific Data Analysis**: The study by LIBR-TU introduces LITURAt, an agent-based system designed to enhance the application of large language models in scientific data analysis. The system addresses challenges of factual accuracy and domain-specific precision by integrating external data retrieval and analysis tools. This approach ensures that outputs are reliable and context-aware, making scientific information more accessible to both experts and nonexperts. The key finding is the ability of agent-based architectures to improve the utility of language models in specialized domains.

2. **Constraining an Unconstrained Multi-agent Policy with Offline Data**: This research focuses on Constrained Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (CMARL), proposing the CUTMAP method. It addresses limitations in online learning by utilizing offline data to impose constraints on multi-agent systems. The methodology involves a scalable optimization objective within a multi-agent maximum entropy reinforcement learning framework, estimating a decomposable Q-function with an unconstrained prior policy. The study highlights the potential of offline data to enhance safety and adaptability in multi-agent systems.

3. **Measuring Water Pollution Effects on Antimicrobial Resistance through Explainable AI**: The study employs explainable AI within the One Health framework to analyze the impact of water quality on antimicrobial resistance. By using a large set of indicators, the research identifies water accessibility and quality as critical factors influencing mortality related to antimicrobial resistance. The findings underscore the role of AI in providing insights for decision support and monitoring in public health.

These articles collectively demonstrate the transformative potential of agent-based frameworks and AI in diverse applications, from scientific data analysis to public health, by enhancing accuracy, adaptability, and decision-making capabilities.
